Have questions? I have answers (maybe).

This Portfolio project was infact very hard for me. I really struggled with the interactive
components. At first I wanted to incorporate a map. I fought endlessly with mapbox and
eventually decided to call it quits. I could not figure out how to properly layer the map then add
the style. After creating it in data base I went into tilesets but the moment I added a
new layer it erased my data. It was incredibly frustrating. I found the timelines to be easy
and straightforward. I really enjoyed writing them. The only thing I did not like however
was that I had to upload my photos onto a website. They are personal family photos and I
cringe at the thought of them being public. When this project has been marked and given back
to me I plan on taking them down from social media. Voyant was straightforward. At first
I was unable to figure out how to embed it properly. I first googled “embed voyant” and
tried a series of codes before realizing there was an export button on the voyant website with
the URL I wanted to use. The pie chart was also very difficult at first. I first calculated by
hand the percentages I wanted to use, then implemented them onto the zingchart website
through the “edit” option. I could not figure out how to share this with github so at first I
saved the edit I had done on my desktop. It did not however allow me to engage with the
project, it more or less saved as a jpeg or pdf. I then downloaded the pie chart and tried to
drag it into the github webpage. This was very time consuming and ineffective. Next I
tried to make a call on the index.html such as “zingchart.html/pie chart”. Again, this was
ineffective. Eventually I learned that to export the URL you had to change information you
wanted on the edit option on zingchart.com then save it in order to export the URL. I
chose to add videos to make the website a little more interactive and was able to find the
codes online without too much distress. The spacing of the images was another issue. It
took a while to find the right code to align the images horizontally rather than vertically
and then to discover the correct spacing between the images so they were all even. I tested
the distance and spaced them evenly. The last issue I had which I am still unable to resolve

was on how to change the text within the buttons. I went through css, css.min, javascript and HTML. I looked up codes, changed all the colors, implemented more codes
and still have been unsuccessful. This was probably the aspect of the project I spent
the most time on. I was able to have the text highlighted when the toggle bar drops down
but I have no clue how I did that and do not know how to change it back. I tried
adjusting my image so that the white would not show but the image did not look as good. For
every photo included in the site I first reshaped then cropped the size on Irfanview.
Learning these programs have improved my understanding of coding, and I believe the
interactive tools I used accentuated the information provided. It was important for me to show
what Jewish culture is/was like prior to the war (voyant), show how many Jewish people
were devastated because of it (pie chart), the events in my grandparents life (timeline) and
why they were significant to the public and my own private life (videos).
