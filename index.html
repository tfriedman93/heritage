About this project

The theme "Bones of our past" has inspired me to write about my trip to Poland this summer with my dad and sister. Poland in the 1930's and 40's was a dangerous places for Jews. My family had to flee in order to survive. This summer my dad, sister, and I visited the town my grandmother grew up in as well as the family who harboured my grandfather during the war. My grandparents were lucky in that they survived. Many of their family members did not however. Some died in concentration camps (such as the infamous Auschwitz-Birkenau) and others location of death is uknown. Many Jews tried to flee before and during the war, burning documents or pieces of evidence which linked them to their Judaism. Because of this, my family is unable to trace most of our family lineage. This project is important because it represents the small amount of information we do have of our family records.
